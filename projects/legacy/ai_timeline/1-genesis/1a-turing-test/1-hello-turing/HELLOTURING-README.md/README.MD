## Key Terms
- Alan Turing: The brilliant mathematician, codebreaker, and visionary thinker who laid the foundation for modern computing and artificial intelligence, and whose life and work continue to inspire and influence researchers and philosophers around the world.
- Turing Machine: The groundbreaking theoretical model of computation proposed by Alan Turing, which demonstrated the fundamental capabilities and limitations of algorithmic processing and paved the way for the development of modern computers.
- Turing Test: The iconic benchmark for evaluating artificial intelligence, proposed by Alan Turing in his seminal 1950 paper, which challenges machines to exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable from that of a human in a text-based conversation.
- Imitation Game: The ingenious thought experiment at the heart of the Turing Test, in which an interrogator engages in a conversation with both a human and a machine, attempting to determine which is which based solely on their responses.
- Intelligence: The elusive and multifaceted concept that lies at the core of the Turing Test and artificial intelligence research, encompassing a wide range of cognitive abilities, from learning and problem-solving to creativity and self-awareness.
- Cognition: The intricate mental processes and capabilities that underlie intelligent behavior, including perception, attention, memory, reasoning, language, and decision-making, which are the focus of study in both human and artificial intelligence research.
